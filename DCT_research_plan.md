## 2. Problem Analysis

### 2.1 The Spatial Lattice: 14 Is Almost Certainly Wrong

**The Kuratowski theorem** states that the monoid generated by one **idempotent**
(closure, k² = k) and one **involution** (complement, c² = id) under the
additional relation `kckckck = kck` has exactly 14 elements. The presentation is:

    M = ⟨ c, k | c² = e, k² = k, kckckck = kck ⟩

**The cohesive modalities** are three endofunctors on the cohesive topos:
- **ʃ** (shape) = Disc ∘ Π  — idempotent monad
- **♭** (flat) = Disc ∘ Γ   — idempotent comonad
- **♯** (sharp) = coDisc ∘ Γ — idempotent monad

These form an adjoint triple: ʃ ⊣ ♭ ⊣ ♯.

**Why 14 fails:** The full faithfulness of Disc and coDisc imposes collapse rules
that have no analogue in the Kuratowski setting:
- Γ ∘ Disc = id  ⟹  ♭ ∘ ʃ = Disc ∘ Γ ∘ Disc ∘ Π = Disc ∘ Π = ʃ
- Π ∘ Disc = id  ⟹  ʃ ∘ ♭ = Disc ∘ Π ∘ Disc ∘ Γ = Disc ∘ Γ = ♭
- Γ ∘ coDisc = id ⟹  ♭ ∘ ♯ = Disc ∘ Γ ∘ coDisc ∘ Γ = Disc ∘ Γ = ♭
- Similarly: ♯ ∘ ♭ = ♯

So compositions of any two adjacent modalities collapse to one of the three.
The monoid generated by {ʃ, ♭, ♯} under composition has **at most ~6 elements**
(id, ʃ, ♭, ♯, ʃ∘♯, ♯∘ʃ), not 14.

**The fundamental mismatch:** Kuratowski's 14 requires an **involution**
(complement: c² = id, non-idempotent). The cohesive modalities are **all
idempotent** (ʃ² = ʃ, ♭² = ♭, ♯² = ♯). No combination of idempotent operators
can generate a Kuratowski-type monoid.

**Possible rescue:** If we include **negation** (¬) from HoTT as an involution,
then ♭ + ¬ might generate a Kuratowski-like monoid. But ¬ is not a cohesive
modality — it's part of the base type theory, available at every step. The paper
specifically attributes the 14 to cohesion alone.

**Alternative interpretation:** Perhaps "14-element lattice" refers not to the
composition monoid but to the **lattice of modal types** — the distinct truth
values under the modalities in a specific model. In the Sierpinski topos,
cohesion can produce richer lattices. This needs investigation.

### 2.2 The Temporal Lattice: 11 Is Likely Wrong

**The claim:** The temporal generators ○ (next) and ◇ (eventually) produce
an 11-element lattice.

**Problem 1: ○ is not idempotent.** Unlike closure, eventually, or the cohesive
modalities, the next-step operator satisfies ○(○(φ)) ≠ ○(φ). This means
○, ○², ○³, ... are all distinct, making the monoid generated by {○, ◇}
**infinite**, not 11.

**Problem 2: ◇ alone + □.** In classical LTL, □ = ¬◇¬ (always). In a
constructive/type-theoretic setting without classical negation, ◇ and □ become
independent. The monoid generated by {◇, □} (both idempotent) with the
absorption laws ◇□◇ = □◇ and □◇□ = ◇□ has exactly **5 elements**:
{id, ◇, □, ◇□, □◇}. Not 11.

**Problem 3: Hamblin's theorem.** The closest classical result is Hamblin's
15-tense theorem: the monoid generated by {F, G, P, H} (future, always, past,
always-past) — **four** idempotent operators with absorption — has 15 elements.
But this requires both future and past operators, which are not part of DCT.

**Possible rescue:** The paper's comment in `GenuineNu.hs` lists 11 elements as:
```
{id, O, O^-1, diamond, square, O.diamond, diamond.O, square.O,
 diamond.square, square.diamond, O^-1.diamond}
```
This includes O⁻¹ (previous step) and both ◇ and □. If O is treated as an
**automorphism** (invertible: O⁻¹ exists) rather than a mere endofunctor, and
if □ is independent of ◇, then we have generators {O, O⁻¹, ◇, □} with the
constraint that O is an automorphism (OO⁻¹ = O⁻¹O = id).

In a **bi-infinite** discrete time model (Z, not N), O is invertible. Then the
monoid structure changes. But even so, the elements O^n for all n ∈ Z make the
monoid infinite unless we impose a periodicity constraint.

The number 11 needs a precise derivation from stated axioms.

### 2.3 The Correction: −4 Is An Estimate

from D adds ≈4. Net: −4."

This is explicitly acknowledged as an estimate (pen_unified.tex:1067). The signs
and magnitudes need rigorous computation once the factor lattices are determined.

## 3. Research Plan

### Phase A: Determine the Correct Spatial Lattice

**Goal:** Compute the exact number of distinct unary type operations generated
by the cohesive modalities, with a rigorous proof.

#### A1. Enumerate the cohesive composition monoid (1-2 days)

Write a Haskell module `SpatialLattice.hs` that:
1. Represents cohesive modality words as compositions of {ʃ, ♭, ♯}
2. Implements the collapse rules from the adjoint quadruple:
   - ʃ² = ʃ, ♭² = ♭, ♯² = ♯ (idempotency)
   - ♭∘ʃ = ʃ, ʃ∘♭ = ♭ (from Γ∘Disc = id, Π∘Disc = id)
   - ♭∘♯ = ♭, ♯∘♭ = ♯ (from Γ∘coDisc = id)
   - ʃ∘♯ = ? and ♯∘ʃ = ? (need to determine from axioms)
3. Enumerates all distinct words by BFS up to length 10
4. Outputs the monoid multiplication table

**Expected result:** 4–7 elements. If it's exactly 7, that matches the
Kuratowski **sub-monoid** generated by {closure, interior} (without complement),
which has presentation ⟨ k, i | k² = k, i² = i, kik = kikik ⟩ and 7 elements.

#### A2. Investigate the role of negation (1 day)

If we include ¬ (type-theoretic negation, available from step 3 onward):
1. Is ¬ an involution on the relevant category? (In HoTT, ¬¬A ≠ A in general,
   so ¬ is NOT an involution unless we restrict to propositions.)
2. On propositions (−1-truncated types), ¬ is an involution if we have LEM.
   Without LEM, ¬¬¬A = ¬A, giving a 3-element chain, not 2.
3. Compute the monoid generated by {♭, ¬} on propositions. Does it yield 14?

**Key question:** Is there any involution in the DCT type theory that, combined
with a cohesive modality, generates a 14-element Kuratowski monoid?

#### A3. Consider differential cohesion (1 day)

In differential cohesion (Schreiber), three additional modalities appear:
- **ℜ** (reduction): idempotent comonad
- **ℑ** (infinitesimal shape): idempotent monad
- **&** (étale): idempotent comonad

These extend the adjoint string. Compute the monoid generated by all 6
modalities {ʃ, ♭, ♯, ℜ, ℑ, &}. With 6 idempotent generators and the
full-faithfulness collapse rules, how many distinct elements are there?

**This is where "14" might actually emerge** — not from Kuratowski, but from
the richer structure of differential cohesion with 6 modalities.

#### A4. Clarify what "operational lattice" means (0.5 days)

The paper says "operational lattice" — is this:
(a) The monoid of endofunctors under composition?
(b) The lattice of modal types (closed/open/discrete/codiscrete/etc.)?
(c) The set of distinct type-level operations (formation + intro + elim rules)?
(d) Something else?

If interpretation (c), then each modality contributes formation + intro + elim =
3 rules. With 4 cohesive operators (♭, ♯, Π, Disc) that's 12 base rules + some
cross-modal interactions. The InferenceNu.hs already computes ν(Cohesion) = 19
via this interpretation. The "lattice" terminology would then be misleading.

### Phase B: Determine the Correct Temporal Lattice

**Goal:** Compute the exact number of distinct unary type operations generated
by the temporal modalities in DCT.

#### B1. Clarify the temporal type theory (1 day)

The DCT has temporal operators ○ (next) and ◇ (eventually). Key questions:
1. Is ○ invertible (does ○⁻¹ exist)? In guarded recursion à la Nakano, ○ has
   no inverse. In bi-infinite time models, it does.
2. Is □ (always) independent or derived? Without classical negation,
   □ ≠ ¬◇¬, so □ must be postulated separately if desired.
3. What are the exact equations? Are ◇ and □ idempotent? Does ○ commute with
   ◇ and □?

Read the key references:
- Nakano (2000): "A modality for recursion" — original guarded recursion
- Clouston et al. (2015-2018): "Guarded dependent type theory" — temporal types
- Bizjak & Møgelberg (2020): "Denotational semantics for guarded dependent type
  theory" — precise semantics of ○ and ◇ in type theory

#### B2. Enumerate the temporal composition monoid (1-2 days)

Write `TemporalLattice.hs`:
1. Generators: {○, ◇} (minimal), or {○, ○⁻¹, ◇, □} (maximal)
2. Relations: ◇² = ◇, □² = □ (if present), ○◇ = ◇○, etc.
3. Key question: if ○ is non-invertible, do we quotient by some equivalence?
   E.g., in guarded recursion, ○ⁿ are all distinct, but maybe for the purpose
   of *type-level* operations, ○² and ○ are equivalent (both "delay")?

If ○ is treated as a genuine non-idempotent non-invertible operator, the monoid
is infinite. **The paper must either:**
- Impose a periodicity/quotient on ○ to make the monoid finite, or
- Redefine "temporal lattice" to exclude ○-powers, or
- Use a different set of generators

#### B3. Investigate the "11 elements" claim (1 day)

The code comment lists:
```
{id, O, O^-1, diamond, square, O.diamond, diamond.O, square.O,
 diamond.square, square.diamond, O^-1.diamond}
```

Attempt to derive this from explicit axioms:
1. If O is an automorphism (O⁻¹ exists, OO⁻¹ = id), then O and O⁻¹ are
   distinct from id but O² is also distinct from O. So either O² must be
   absent or equal to one of the listed elements.
2. Check: does diamond.O.diamond = diamond? (absorption)
3. Check: does O.O equal something in the list?
4. Systematically verify closure of the 11-element set under composition.

If the 11 elements are closed under composition, produce the multiplication
table as proof. If not, identify which compositions fall outside the set.

### Phase C: Compute the Tensor Product

**Goal:** Given the actual spatial lattice S and temporal lattice T, compute the
tensor product S ⊗ T modulo the compatibility axioms C1-C3.

#### C1. Formalize the compatibility triad (1 day)

The DCT compatibility axioms are:
- (C1) ○(♭X) ≃ ♭(○X)  — next commutes with flat
- (C2) ○(ΠX) ≃ Π(○X)  — next commutes with shape
- (C3) ○(X^D) ≃ (○X)^D — next commutes with infinitesimal

These say that ○ commutes with the spatial modalities. In the tensor product
framework, these are the relations that identify certain elements of S × T.

#### C2. Compute the tensor product algebraically (1-2 days)

If |S| = s and |T| = t, the raw product has s × t elements. The compatibility
axioms identify pairs (σ, τ) and (σ', τ') whenever σ∘τ = σ'∘τ' in the
combined monoid. Count the equivalence classes.

Implementation:
1. Build the S × T monoid (direct product)
2. Impose the commutativity relations from C1-C3
3. Compute the quotient
4. Count distinct elements

#### C3. Account for infinitesimals (0.5 days)

The infinitesimal type D adds the tangent operation X ↦ X^D. This is another
endofunctor. Determine:
1. Is X ↦ X^D idempotent? (Probably not: (X^D)^D is the second jet bundle,
   distinct from X^D = first jet bundle.)
2. How does X^D interact with the spatial and temporal modalities beyond C3?
3. Does the infinitesimal contribution add or subtract from the raw count?

## 4. Alternative Approaches to ν(DCT) = 150

If the composition-monoid interpretation gives a number much smaller than 150,
the paper's "semantic audit" table (Appendix A.5) suggests an alternative
interpretation: ν counts not the monoid elements but the **distinct mathematical
constructions** enabled by the DCT synthesis. The audit lists 150 constructions
across 14 domains (dynamical systems, mechanics, quantum, PDE, gauge theory,
etc.).

Under this interpretation, ν(DCT) = 150 might be correct for the wrong reason:
it's not a lattice tensor product but a count of distinct inference rules
enabled by the interaction of spatial, temporal, and infinitesimal structure.

This would require:
1. Abandoning the "Lattice Tensor Product" theorem as stated
2. Replacing it with an explicit enumeration of inference rules (consistent
   with the Generative Capacity definition)
3. Justifying why the list has 150 entries and not more or fewer

This fallback is scientifically honest but weaker than a clean algebraic proof.

## 7. Risk Assessment

**High risk:** Both factor counts (14, 11) appear to be incorrect as stated.
The 14 conflates Kuratowski's closure-complement monoid (which requires an
involution) with the cohesive modality monoid (which has only idempotents).
The 11 assumes a finite monoid for {○, ◇}, but ○ is non-idempotent, making
the monoid infinite.

**Mitigation:** The theory does NOT require ν(DCT) = exactly 150. It requires
ν(DCT)/κ(DCT) > Bar₁₅ ≈ 7.25, i.e., ν(DCT) > 58. If the actual lattice
product is, say, 80 or 120, the singularity still occurs — just with different
algebraic justification.

**Best case:** The correct interpretation of "operational lattice" (perhaps
including differential cohesion's 6 modalities, or counting inference rules
rather than monoid elements) yields a number close to 150 with a clean
algebraic proof.

**Worst case:** The correct count is < 58, falsifying the Genesis Sequence.
This would be a genuine scientific result — the theory makes a prediction
and the prediction fails.
