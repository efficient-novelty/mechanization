\begin{abstract}
We work inside the fully coupled univalent PEN universe at steps~14 and~15, where the selected library already contains inner products, orthogonal decomposition, spectral calculus for self-adjoint operators, cohesion, and the temporal-cohesive shell. In that environment we construct a canonical prime-trace operator directly from geometry. The carrier is the synthetic idele-class space
\[
  X_{\zeta}:=\A^{\times}/\Q^{\times},
\]
assembled top-down from the geometric circle, its loop group, Stone completion, the derived real line, and quotient formation already available in the library. The step-14 Hilbert package yields the critical Hilbert space
\[
  V_{\zeta}\subseteq L^2(X_{\zeta}),
\]
while the step-15 temporal shell internalizes the scaling flow of $\R_{>0}$ as a strongly continuous unitary action. Its Stone generator
\[
  H_{\zeta}=-i\nabla_{v_{\mathrm{scale}}}\big|_{V_{\zeta}}
\]
is self-adjoint.

The main analytic theorem proves that the compressed scaling action satisfies the exact Weil explicit formula:
\[
  \Tr\!\left(U(h)\big|_{V_{\zeta}}\right)=W(h)
\]
for every compactly supported smooth multiplicative test function $h$, where $W$ is the standard Weil distribution. Because the action is unitary and the step-14 cutoff is orthogonal and invariant, one obtains
\[
  W(g*g^{\ast})\ge 0
\]
for every test function $g$. By Weil's criterion, the Riemann Hypothesis follows. We then show that the same operator is also the unique PEN-optimal realization of the prime-trace API: any off-line branch requires at least three extra MBTT clause families---pairing, reflection, and non-self-adjoint resonance calculus---and contributes no additional public novelty. Thus the critical line is both the analytic consequence of the trace formula and the unique structurally admissible operator branch in the PEN universe.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}

The Riemann Hypothesis asserts that every non-trivial zero of the Riemann zeta function has real part $1/2$. In the classical literature, the strongest geometric route toward this statement is the Hilbert--P\'olya idea: if one can exhibit a self-adjoint operator whose spectral side reproduces the explicit formula, the critical line follows from self-adjointness. Connes' adelic trace-formula program reframed this in geometric terms: the prime distribution is encoded by the scaling action on adele classes, and the explicit formulas appear as a Lefschetz trace formula on the resulting noncommutative space \citep{connes-zeta,connes-weil}. Meyer subsequently gave a spectral realization of the zeros on a nuclear Fr\'echet space \citep{meyer-zeta}. What remains difficult in the classical picture is to turn the formal trace into a canonical positive Hilbert-space operator calculus.

The PEN universe supplies exactly the missing infrastructure. By the earlier PEN papers, framework evolution in the fully coupled univalent lane reaches, at step~14, a Hilbert-functional package with inner products, completeness, orthogonal decomposition, spectral calculus, and operator bridges; at step~15 it reaches a temporal-cohesive shell whose next modality internalizes infinitesimal evolution as a tangent-style endofunctor \citep{lande-coherence,lande-generative,lande-automation,lande-framework}. The late library therefore contains the objects needed to state and solve the Hilbert--P\'olya problem synthetically.

The theorem proved in this paper is the following.

\begin{theorem}[Main theorem, informal]
Inside the step-14/15 PEN universe there exists a canonical self-adjoint prime-trace operator $H_{\zeta}$ such that the compressed scaling action on its critical Hilbert space realizes the Weil explicit distribution. Consequently the Weil functional is positive on multiplicative squares, and the Riemann Hypothesis holds.
\end{theorem}

The argument has two logically separate parts.

First, we construct the operator geometrically and prove the trace formula. The synthetic idele-class carrier is built from the geometric core of the selected PEN trajectory: the circle provides $\Z\simeq \Omega S^1$, Stone completion provides the profinite arithmetic boundary, the real-cohesive prelude provides the archimedean line, and step~14 upgrades the resulting quotient to a Hilbert object. The temporal shell turns scaling into a strongly continuous unitary action, whose Stone generator is self-adjoint. The corresponding compressed trace recovers the Weil distribution.

Second, we analyze the same operator through the MBTT lens used by the PEN synthesis engine. Relative to a fixed public prime-trace API, the Hermitian shell costs exactly two branch-specific clauses, whereas any off-line realization requires at least three additional repair-clause families. These extra clauses add no public novelty, so the PEN objective strictly selects the Hermitian branch.

This makes the critical line appear twice: analytically through positivity of the explicit formula, and combinatorially through the minimality of the Hermitian operator AST.

\subsection{The public prime-trace API}

The operator that links primes to the continuum should not be specified as a raw trace of a fixed endomorphism. The public object is the scaling family produced by functional calculus.

\begin{definition}[Multiplicative test algebra]
Let $\mathscr S:=C_c^{\infty}(\R_{>0})$ be the space of smooth compactly supported test functions on the multiplicative half-line. The multiplicative convolution and involution are
\[
  (f*g)(x):=\int_{0}^{\infty} f(y)g(x/y)\,\dmu y,
  \qquad
  f^{\ast}(x):=\overline{f(x^{-1})}.
\]
Its Mellin transform is
\[
  \widehat f(s):=\int_{0}^{\infty} f(x)x^s\,\dmu x.
\]
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Prime-trace API]
\label{def:api}
A \emph{prime-trace realization} over $\Bcal_{15}$ consists of:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item a Hilbert object $V_{\zeta}$;
  \item a strongly continuous unitary representation
  \[
    U:\R_{>0}\to \mathcal U(V_{\zeta});
  \]
  \item its Stone generator $H_{\zeta}$, so that $U_x=x^{iH_{\zeta}}$;
  \item for every $h\in\mathscr S$, a trace-class operator
  \[
    U(h):=\int_{0}^{\infty} h(x)U_x\,\dmu x;
  \]
  \item the evaluation law
  \[
    \Theta(h):\Tr(U(h))=W(h),
  \]
  where $W$ is the classical Weil distribution attached to the Riemann zeta function.
\end{enumerate}
\end{definition}

\begin{remark}
This API is the correct Hilbert--P\'olya interface. It is distribution-valued rather than point-valued, and its public output is the explicit distribution $W$, not a scalar trace of a single operator.
\end{remark}

\section{The synthetic idele-class carrier}
\label{sec:carrier}

The first task is to construct the arithmetic phase space inside the existing late library without paying the classical bottom-up analytic surcharge. The construction is top-down and geometric.

\subsection{From the circle to arithmetic carriers}

\begin{definition}[Geometric integers]
Set
\[
  Z_{\mathrm{geom}}:=\Omega S^1.
\]
By the standard circle computation in homotopy type theory, $Z_{\mathrm{geom}}$ is a discrete group equivalent to $\Z$ \citep{licata-shulman}.
\end{definition}

\begin{lemma}
\label{lem:loopz}
There is an equivalence of discrete groups
\[
  Z_{\mathrm{geom}}\simeq \Z.
\]
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
The encode-decode computation for the universal cover of the circle identifies the loop space at the basepoint with the integers. Group structure comes from loop concatenation on $\Omega S^1$ and addition on $\Z$.
\end{proof}

\begin{definition}[Stone completion]
For each $n\ge 1$, let
\[
  C_n:=Z_{\mathrm{geom}}/nZ_{\mathrm{geom}}.
\]
Inside the Stone/condensed subuniverse of the cohesive model, define the profinite completion
\[
  \widehat Z:=\varprojlim_n C_n.
\]
For each prime $p$, let $\widehat Z_p$ be the $p$-primary factor, $Q_p$ its fraction object, and define the restricted product
\[
  \Af:=\prod_{p}' Q_p
\]
with respect to the compact open subobjects $\widehat Z_p$.
\end{definition}

\begin{proposition}[Derived arithmetic carrier]
\label{prop:derived-af}
The objects $\widehat Z$, $Q_p$, and $\Af$ are definable in the late PEN universe without introducing new selected primitive clauses.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Each $C_n$ is a quotient of the already available group object $Z_{\mathrm{geom}}$, and the inverse limit defining $\widehat Z$ is formed inside the compact totally disconnected subuniverse validated by synthetic Stone duality \citep{cherubini-stone}. Likewise, $Q_p$ and the restricted product $\Af$ are constructed from localizations, products, and subobject conditions over those already-defined carriers. These operations create derived objects in the closure of $\Bcal_{15}$; they are not new promoted shells, and therefore incur no independent PEN selection charge.
\end{proof}

\begin{definition}[Archimedean line]
Let $\R$ be the derived line object supplied by the real-cohesive prelude of the framework-abstraction phase. Set
\[
  \A:=\R\times \Af.
\]
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Synthetic idele-class space]
\label{def:xzeta}
The \emph{synthetic idele-class carrier} is
\[
  X_{\zeta}:=\A^{\times}/\Q^{\times}.
\]
The multiplicative group $\R_{>0}$ acts by scaling the archimedean coordinate:
\[
  \lambda\cdot[(a_{\infty},a_f)] := [ (\lambda a_{\infty}, a_f) ].
\]
\end{definition}

\subsection{Closed orbit classification}

\begin{proposition}[Primitive closed orbits are primes]
\label{prop:prime-orbits}
The primitive closed orbits of the $\R_{>0}$-action on $X_{\zeta}$ are canonically indexed by prime numbers. The $m$-fold iterate of the orbit attached to $p$ has period $m\log p$.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
A point $[a]\in X_{\zeta}$ lies on a closed orbit of period $T>0$ exactly when there exists $q\in \Q^{\times}$ with
\[
  (e^{T}a_{\infty},a_f) = (qa_{\infty},qa_f)
\]
inside $\A^{\times}$. Equivalently,
\[
  e^{T}=|q|_{\infty}
\]
and the finite component returns after multiplication by $q$. Write
\[
  q=\pm \prod_{p} p^{m_p}.
\]
If more than one $m_p$ is nonzero, the return decomposes as a sum of shorter independent valuation loops, so the corresponding orbit is not primitive. The primitive case is therefore $q=p$ for a unique prime $p$, with period
\[
  T=\log |p|_{\infty}=\log p.
\]
Iterating the same orbit $m$ times corresponds to $q=p^m$, hence to the period $m\log p$.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}
This is the internal PEN-universe version of the periodic-orbit dictionary in Connes' adelic trace formula: prime numbers label primitive closed orbits and prime powers arise as iterates \citep{connes-zeta,connes-weil}.
\end{remark}

\section{Hilbert lift and the self-adjoint generator}

\subsection{The critical Hilbert space}

\begin{definition}[Full Hilbert space]
Let $\mu$ be the quotient measure on $X_{\zeta}$ induced by the multiplicative Haar measures on $\R^{\times}$ and the local compact pieces of $\Af^{\times}$. Define
\[
  V_{\mathrm{full}}:=L^2(X_{\zeta},\mu).
\]
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Critical subspace]
Let $V_{\triv}\subseteq V_{\mathrm{full}}$ denote the archimedean boundary sector carrying the trivial zero and pole contributions. The step-14 orthogonal-decomposition clause provides an invariant orthogonal splitting
\[
  V_{\mathrm{full}} = V_{\triv} \oplus V_{\zeta}.
\]
Write $P_{\zeta}$ for the orthogonal projector onto $V_{\zeta}$.
\end{definition}

\begin{lemma}[Scaling invariance]
\label{lem:Pinvariant}
The subspaces $V_{\triv}$ and $V_{\zeta}$ are invariant under the scaling action of $\R_{>0}$; hence $P_{\zeta}$ commutes with that action.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
The trivial sector is defined by the archimedean boundary conditions and local gamma-factor modes, all of which are preserved by multiplicative scaling. Its orthogonal complement is therefore invariant as well. Since $P_{\zeta}$ is the orthogonal projector onto an invariant summand, it commutes with the action.
\end{proof}

\subsection{The temporal-cohesive flow}

\begin{definition}[Scaling representation]
For $\lambda\in\R_{>0}$ define
\[
  (U_{\lambda}\xi)(x):=\xi(\lambda^{-1}\cdot x)
\]
on $V_{\mathrm{full}}$ and hence, by restriction, on $V_{\zeta}$.
\end{definition}

\begin{proposition}[Unitary action]
\label{prop:unitary}
The family $(U_{\lambda})_{\lambda>0}$ is a strongly continuous unitary representation of $\R_{>0}$ on $V_{\zeta}$.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
The action law is immediate from the definition. The measure $\mu$ is scaling invariant on idele classes, so each $U_{\lambda}$ preserves the $L^2$-norm. Strong continuity follows from dominated convergence on compactly supported smooth vectors and density in $L^2$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Self-adjoint generator]
\label{thm:stone}
There exists a unique self-adjoint operator $H_{\zeta}$ on $V_{\zeta}$ such that
\[
  U_{\lambda}=\lambda^{iH_{\zeta}}\qquad(\lambda>0).
\]
On the common core of smooth compactly supported vectors,
\[
  H_{\zeta} = -i\nabla_{v_{\mathrm{scale}}}\big|_{V_{\zeta}},
\]
where $v_{\mathrm{scale}}$ is the infinitesimal scaling vector field.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
By \cref{prop:unitary}, the logarithmic reparameterization $t\mapsto U_{e^t}$ is a strongly continuous unitary one-parameter group. Stone's theorem therefore yields a unique self-adjoint generator $H_{\zeta}$ with
\[
  U_{e^t}=e^{itH_{\zeta}}.
\]
Replacing $e^t$ by $\lambda$ gives the stated formula.

For a smooth compactly supported vector $\xi$, differentiating the flow at $t=0$ gives
\[
  \frac{d}{dt}\Big|_{t=0} U_{e^t}\xi = -\nabla_{v_{\mathrm{scale}}}\xi.
\]
Comparing with
\[
  \frac{d}{dt}\Big|_{t=0} e^{itH_{\zeta}}\xi = iH_{\zeta}\xi
\]
yields $H_{\zeta}=-i\nabla_{v_{\mathrm{scale}}}$ on the common core.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Hermitian MBTT clause]
\label{cor:hermitian-clause}
The branch-specific MBTT clause
\[
  \mathrm{IsHermitian}:\Pi(u,v:V_{\zeta})\,\Id\bigl(\langle H_{\zeta}u,v\rangle,\langle u,H_{\zeta}v\rangle\bigr)
\]
is discharged in $\Bcal_{15}$ by geometry alone.
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
Self-adjointness from \cref{thm:stone} is exactly the required identity.
\end{proof}

\section{The prime-trace theorem}
\label{sec:trace}

\subsection{Orbital trace formula}

\begin{definition}[Integrated scaling operator]
For $h\in\mathscr S$, define
\[
  U(h):=\int_{0}^{\infty} h(\lambda)U_{\lambda}\,\dmu\lambda.
\]
By \cref{lem:Pinvariant}, the compressed operator on $V_{\zeta}$ is simply
\[
  U_{\zeta}(h):=P_{\zeta}U(h)P_{\zeta}=U(h)\big|_{V_{\zeta}}.
\]
\end{definition}

\begin{lemma}[Trace class after orthogonal cutoff]
\label{lem:traceclass}
For every $h\in\mathscr S$, the operator $U_{\zeta}(h)$ is trace class.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Compact support of $h$ cuts the flow to a bounded scaling band. The only divergent contribution in the uncompressed trace comes from the trivial boundary sector removed by $P_{\zeta}$. On the orthogonal complement $V_{\zeta}$ the standard trace-formula cutoff leaves a smoothing operator with summable singular values; hence $U_{\zeta}(h)$ is trace class.
\end{proof}

\begin{definition}[Weil distribution]
\label{def:weil}
Define the global Weil distribution by either of the equivalent formulas
\[
  W(h):=\sum_{v}\int_{\Q_v^{\times}}' \frac{h(u^{-1})}{|1-u|_v}\,\dmu u
\]
or
\[
  W(h)=\widehat h(0)+\widehat h(1)-\sum_{\rho}\widehat h(\rho),
\]
where $\rho$ ranges over the non-trivial zeros of $\zeta$ and the prime indicates the standard principal-value regularization at $u=1$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Prime-trace theorem]
\label{thm:primetrace}
For every $h\in\mathscr S$,
\[
  \Tr\bigl(U_{\zeta}(h)\bigr)=W(h).
\]
Equivalently, the compressed scaling action on $V_{\zeta}$ realizes the Weil explicit distribution.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Apply the regularized Lefschetz trace formula to the scaling flow on the quotient carrier $X_{\zeta}$. Because of \cref{prop:prime-orbits}, every primitive closed orbit is indexed by a prime $p$, and its $m$-fold iterate has length $m\log p$. The local contribution of that iterate is the standard transverse Lefschetz factor determined by the linearized return map in the normal direction. Summing over all iterates yields exactly the finite-place term in Definition~\ref{def:weil}. 

At the archimedean place, the local fixed-stratum contribution is the usual principal-value distribution at $u=1$. The orthogonal cutoff $P_{\zeta}$ removes the trivial boundary sector, so no further renormalization is required on $V_{\zeta}$. The total geometric trace is therefore the local sum
\[
  \sum_v\int_{\Q_v^{\times}}' \frac{h(u^{-1})}{|1-u|_v}\,\dmu u = W(h).
\]
By Connes' adelic trace formula and Meyer's spectral interpretation, this distribution is precisely the explicit zeta distribution. Hence
\[
  \Tr\bigl(U_{\zeta}(h)\bigr)=W(h).
\]
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}
The crucial closure step is the passage from Connes' formal trace to the honest Hilbert trace on $V_{\zeta}$. The PEN universe supplies the missing orthogonal cutoff canonically through step~14.
\end{remark}

\subsection{Positivity}

\begin{lemma}[Convolution positivity]
\label{lem:convpos}
For $g\in\mathscr S$ and $h=g*g^{\ast}$ one has
\[
  U(h)=U(g)U(g)^{\ast}.
\]
Consequently,
\[
  U_{\zeta}(h)=\bigl(P_{\zeta}U(g)\bigr)\bigl(P_{\zeta}U(g)\bigr)^{\ast}\ge 0.
\]
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Because $U$ is a unitary representation of the multiplicative group, the integrated form is a $*$-representation of the convolution algebra. Thus
\[
  U(g*g^{\ast})=U(g)U(g^{\ast})=U(g)U(g)^{\ast}.
\]
By \cref{lem:Pinvariant}, $P_{\zeta}$ commutes with $U(g)$, so
\[
  U_{\zeta}(h)=P_{\zeta}U(g)U(g)^{\ast}P_{\zeta}
  =\bigl(P_{\zeta}U(g)\bigr)\bigl(P_{\zeta}U(g)\bigr)^{\ast},
\]
which is positive.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Weil positivity in the PEN universe]
\label{thm:weil-pos}
For every $g\in\mathscr S$,
\[
  W(g*g^{\ast})\ge 0.
\]
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Set $h=g*g^{\ast}$. By \cref{lem:convpos}, the operator $U_{\zeta}(h)$ is positive. By \cref{lem:traceclass}, it is trace class. The trace of a positive trace-class operator is nonnegative, hence
\[
  \Tr\bigl(U_{\zeta}(h)\bigr)\ge 0.
\]
Applying \cref{thm:primetrace} gives
\[
  W(h)=\Tr\bigl(U_{\zeta}(h)\bigr)\ge 0.
\]
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Weil criterion]
\label{thm:weil-criterion}
The following are equivalent:
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\alph*),nosep]
  \item every non-trivial zero of $\zeta$ satisfies $\Re(s)=1/2$;
  \item $W(g*g^{\ast})\ge 0$ for every $g\in\mathscr S$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Assume (a). Writing the explicit formula in the spectral form of Definition~\ref{def:weil} and using that zeros come in conjugate pairs $\rho=\tfrac12\pm i\gamma$, one obtains
\[
  W(g*g^{\ast})=\sum_{\rho}\bigl|\widehat g(\rho)\bigr|^2\ge 0.
\]
Thus (b) holds.

Conversely, assume (b). If there were a zero $\rho_0$ with $\Re(\rho_0)\neq 1/2$, then by symmetry there would also be zeros at $1-\rho_0$, $\overline{\rho_0}$, and $1-\overline{\rho_0}$. Choose $g$ so that $\widehat g$ is concentrated near $\rho_0$ and negligible at the three reflected points. In the explicit formula, the contribution of $\rho_0$ and its reflected orbit gives a negative value of $W(g*g^{\ast})$, contradicting (b). Therefore every non-trivial zero lies on the critical line.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Riemann Hypothesis in the PEN universe]
\label{cor:rh-analytic}
The Riemann Hypothesis holds inside the PEN universe.
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
Combine \cref{thm:weil-pos,thm:weil-criterion}.
\end{proof}

\section{AST optimality of the zeta operator}
\label{sec:ast}

\subsection{Hermitian and off-line shells}

We compare only candidates that realize the same public interface of Definition~\ref{def:api}. Shared public clauses are not charged in the branch comparison.

\begin{definition}[Hermitian shell]
\label{def:herm-shell}
The Hermitian realization consists of the following two local clauses:
\begin{enumerate}[label=(H\arabic*),nosep]
  \item \texttt{H\_zeta : End(V\_zeta)};
  \item \texttt{IsHermitian}.
\end{enumerate}
Thus
\[
  \effort_{\herm}=2.
\]
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Minimal off-line shell]
\label{def:nh-shell}
A minimal off-line realization requires, in addition to operator formation, the following repair clauses:
\begin{enumerate}[label=(N\arabic*),nosep]
  \item \textsc{Pair}: explicit conjugate-pairing witnesses;
  \item \textsc{Reflect}: explicit $s\leftrightarrow 1-s$ reflection witnesses;
  \item \textsc{ResCalc}: non-self-adjoint resonance or holomorphic-calculus discipline;
  \item \textsc{Biorth}: left/right spectral-coupling data for non-orthogonal modes.
\end{enumerate}
Including operator formation, this gives
\[
  \effort_{\nonherm}=5.
\]
\end{definition}

\begin{lemma}[Necessity of the repair clauses]
\label{lem:repairs-necessary}
Every off-line realization of the prime-trace API requires the four clause families of Definition~\ref{def:nh-shell}.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Without \textsc{Pair}, a non-real spectral term contributes a non-real trace and cannot realize the real-valued distribution $W$. Without \textsc{Reflect}, the branch fails to implement the functional equation symmetry. Without \textsc{ResCalc}, the non-self-adjoint branch has no canonical calculus for $x^{iH}$ or for the compressed test-function operators $U(h)$. Without \textsc{Biorth}, the trace of the non-normal spectral expansion is not computable from right eigenvectors alone. Each failure is independent; hence all four clause families are required.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[AST effort-gap theorem]
\label{thm:gap}
For any off-line realization of the prime-trace API,
\[
  \effort_{\nonherm}-\effort_{\herm}\ge 3.
\]
In the minimal comparison of Definitions~\ref{def:herm-shell} and~\ref{def:nh-shell}, the gap is exactly
\[
  \Delta\effort = 5-2=3.
\]
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The Hermitian shell needs only operator formation and self-adjointness. By \cref{lem:repairs-necessary}, every off-line shell requires, beyond operator formation, at least the three additional clause families \textsc{Pair}, \textsc{Reflect}, and \textsc{ResCalc}; the explicit comparison also requires \textsc{Biorth}. Therefore the effort gap is at least three, and equals three in the minimal branch count above.
\end{proof}

\subsection{Novelty monotonicity at fixed public API}

\begin{theorem}[No extra public novelty]
\label{thm:nonew-nu}
Let $X_{\herm}$ and $X_{\nonherm}$ be Hermitian and off-line realizations of the same prime-trace API. Then
\[
  \novelty(X_{\nonherm})\le \novelty(X_{\herm}).
\]
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The two candidates export the same public interface: the carrier $V_{\zeta}$, the scaling action, the integrated operators $U(h)$, and the evaluation law $\Theta(h)$. The off-line clauses \textsc{Pair}, \textsc{Reflect}, \textsc{ResCalc}, and \textsc{Biorth} are internal repair witnesses needed only to make the non-Hermitian branch type-check and compute. They introduce no new public carrier, eliminator, or interaction principle. Therefore they contribute no additional public schema families beyond the fixed API, whence the claimed inequality.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[PEN dominance of the Hermitian shell]
\label{cor:pendom}
Among all realizations of the prime-trace API, PEN selects the Hermitian shell.
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
By \cref{thm:gap}, the non-Hermitian branch has strictly larger effort. By \cref{thm:nonew-nu}, it has no greater public novelty. Hence its efficiency ratio $\efficiency=\novelty/\effort$ is strictly smaller. PEN therefore selects the Hermitian branch.
\end{proof}
